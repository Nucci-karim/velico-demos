How it works

Three steps, unchanged for forty years

Pick your fish

Walk to the counter and choose from the day's catch: kingfish, sheri, hamour, prawns.

Pay by the kilo

Your fish goes on the scale. You pay for weight, not for decor.

Masala, fire, paratha

Marinated in the house masala and fried, served with paratha, rice and coconut curry sauce.

A full meal usually lands around AED 50–90 a head. First come, first served.

The story

From pearl divers to Dubai's worst-kept secret

Bu Qtair was opened in the 1980s by Matar Al Tayer, from an Emirati family of fishermen and pearl divers: a shack by Jumeirah beach, named after the coastal spots where divers washed in sweet water.

For four years, almost nobody came. Then the cook, Mousa, started frying the fishermen's own fresh catch. Word of mouth did the rest: today the queue at the harbour includes taxi drivers and high rollers alike.

Anthony Bourdain came to Dubai and ate here: deep-fried grouper and prawns with coconut curry and paratha.
